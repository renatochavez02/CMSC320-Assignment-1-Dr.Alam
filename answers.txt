Q3: What is the difference between merging and rebasing?
Answer: Merging combines the changes from one branch to the other, while rebasing reapplies commits from certain branch into another base branch and rewrites the commit history. In other words, merging is best use when we integrate sub branches into the main branch and preserve the history of all branches, while rebasing maintains a linear history of the main branch.
